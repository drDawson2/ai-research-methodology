Why Version Tracking Matters

Living Methodology Principle

This project documents a methodology that develops alongside active dissertation research. Version tracking serves multiple purposes:

Transparency

Shows the methodology evolves through use, not perfect design. Demonstrates iterative refinement central to action research. Documents what changes, when, and why.

Accountability

Public versioning creates pressure to maintain quality. Changes must be justified and documented.

Replicability

Others can see progression from foundation to complex system. Clear documentation of what exists at each stage. Ability to cite specific versions in their own work.

Reflexivity

Tracking versions forces reflection on what's working. Documents learning alongside implementation. Shows researcher development over time.

Honesty

Not pretending this emerged fully-formed. Acknowledging false starts and adjustments. Modeling realistic research development.
