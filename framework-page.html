Academic Integrity Framework

Maintaining Scholarly Rigor in AI-Assisted Research

This framework establishes clear boundaries for ethical AI collaboration in doctoral research. It's built on years of professional facilitation experience and tested through actual dissertation preparation work.

Core principle: AI augments your scholarship‚Äîit doesn't replace it.

Four Bright Line Tests

If you can't confidently answer YES to all four questions, reassess how you're using AI.

Advisor Transparency Test

"Would I be comfortable showing this chat to my advisor?"

  • ‚úì YES = You're using AI for legitimate support
  • ‚úó NO = You may be crossing into inappropriate territory

If you're hiding AI conversations, ask yourself why. Discomfort signals potential integrity issues.

Independent Understanding Test

"Can I explain my work without referencing the AI chat?"

  • ‚úì YES = You understand your work independently
  • ‚úó NO = You're relying on AI's thinking, not your own

If you can only explain concepts by saying "Claude said that..." you don't truly understand them.

Source Engagement Test

"Did I personally read all sources I'm citing?"

  • ‚úì YES = Your scholarship is grounded in primary sources
  • ‚úó NO = You're using AI summaries instead of reading

AI cannot read sources for you. Every citation must be based on YOUR direct engagement with the text.

Authorship Test

"Did I write all dissertation content myself?"

  • ‚úì YES = You maintain full intellectual ownership
  • ‚úó NO = AI is ghostwriting your work

Every sentence in your dissertation must be YOUR words, YOUR analysis, YOUR arguments.

Clear Boundaries: What's Appropriate and What's Not

‚úì APPROPRIATE AI USES

Literature Work

  • Asking synthesis questions about sources YOU'VE read
  • Getting frameworks for organizing YOUR notes
  • Identifying gaps in YOUR analysis
  • Testing YOUR interpretations through dialogue

Theoretical Framework

  • Discussing integration logic between theories
  • Preparing for committee questions
  • Mapping YOUR theories to YOUR methods
  • Testing coherence of YOUR arguments

Data Analysis

  • Discussing coding dilemmas (with de-identified excerpts)
  • Refining code definitions YOU'VE drafted
  • Testing pattern recognition YOU'VE noticed
  • Generating alternative interpretations to consider

Writing & Organization

  • Getting structural suggestions for YOUR drafts
  • Checking argument coherence
  • Receiving transition suggestions
  • Identifying unclear sections

‚úó INAPPROPRIATE AI USES

Literature Work

  • Asking AI to summarize sources instead of reading them
  • Using AI-generated literature reviews
  • Copying AI synthesis into your dissertation
  • Having AI write "your" interpretation of sources

Theoretical Framework

  • Asking AI to "write my theoretical framework"
  • Copying AI explanations of theory relationships
  • Using AI to decide which theories to use
  • Claiming AI-generated frameworks as your thinking

Data Analysis

  • Having AI code your entire dataset
  • Sharing identifiable participant data
  • Using AI's interpretations as your findings
  • Letting AI make analytical decisions for you

Writing

  • Asking AI to write dissertation sections
  • Copying AI-generated paragraphs directly
  • Using AI to write findings or discussion chapters
  • Having AI generate "your" arguments

Real Examples: Integrity in Action

These examples from this project's development show the framework applied in practice.

Example 1: Maintaining Authorship During Synthesis (Session 6)

Situation: During theoretical synthesis work, Claude provided example language showing "what kind of specificity would work."

Researcher's Response: Immediately asked: "Is what you're suggesting a significant enough shift that the content shifts from being mine to yours? What is the ratio of human to AI?"

Resolution: Chose question-based approach instead: "I would like to do that but by responding to prompting questions from you... I will draft a response that is intended to be part of the post and we can edit it together."

Why This Maintains Integrity:

  • Caught potential boundary issue before using content
  • Explicitly chose approach preserving authorship
  • Drafted all synthesis language in own words
  • Maintained intellectual ownership

Example 2: Simple Edit Recognition (Real-Time Self-Direction)

Situation: Researcher needed to change name on presentation slide with 20-minute notice before advisor meeting.

Initial impulse: "Can you change the name on the first slide?"

Self-correction: Researcher realized this was a simple edit (Ctrl+F in HTML code), paused the request, and made the change independently.

Why This Matters:

  • Scaffolded support ‚Üí independent capability development
  • Living the research question (dependency ‚Üí self-direction)
  • Appropriate boundary-setting (not over-relying for convenience)
  • Building transferable skills rather than maintaining dependence
