Session 4: Academic Integrity Framework Discussion

November 1, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 45 minutes ‚Ä¢ Phase: Meta

Quick Summary: Preparing for theory synthesis work. Researcher raised proactive concern about how collaboration might be perceived by advisor/reviewers.

Key Output: Four bright line tests, appropriate vs. inappropriate use framework

Integrity: Proactive ethics discussion demonstrates researcher actively monitoring integrity

Click to expand full details ‚ñº

Researcher's Question

"How might these theory and synthesis chats look in the log? Could someone think synthesis and literature review should be original work and that using AI means not doing the work?"

Why Asked

Proactive concern about academic integrity. Wanted to ensure AI collaboration is defensible and not inadvertently crossing ethical boundaries. Needed clarity on appropriate vs. inappropriate use.

Four Bright Line Tests Developed

  1. Advisor transparency: Would I be comfortable showing this chat?
  2. Independent understanding: Can I explain without the chat?
  3. Source engagement: Did I read sources myself?
  4. Authorship: Did I write dissertation content myself?

Core Distinction Established

  • ‚úì Appropriate: AI as thinking partner (dialogue deepens understanding, researcher writes)
  • ‚úó Inappropriate: AI as ghostwriter (AI generates content, researcher copies)

Process That Maintains Integrity

Read independently ‚Üí Synthesis dialogue ‚Üí Write independently

Decision

Proceed with theory/literature chats as planned. Process is academically appropriate because researcher reads sources, develops interpretations, asks questions (not requests content), and writes dissertation independently.

Significance

Proactive ethics discussion demonstrates researcher is actively monitoring integrity, not blindly using AI.

Session 5: AI Limitations Discussion - Date Confusion

November 1, 2025 ‚Ä¢ 20 minutes ‚Ä¢ Phase: Meta

Quick Summary: Despite researcher repeatedly stating "today is Saturday, November 1," Claude kept getting confused. Discussion revealed AI limitations and importance of researcher maintaining ground truth.

Key Output: Understanding of AI capabilities/limitations, orientation protocol thinking

Integrity: Critical engagement with AI outputs strengthens methodology

Click to expand full details ‚ñº

What Happened

Despite researcher repeatedly stating correct date, Claude kept trying to calculate backward from context clues and questioning direct statements about the current date.

What This Revealed About AI Collaboration

AI is reliable for:

Analyzing text researcher provides
Providing frameworks and structures
Offering synthesis questions
Identifying patterns across sources
Giving feedback on drafts

AI is NOT reliable for:

  • Remembering when conversations happened
  • Tracking time between check-ins
  • Knowing what researcher did when not talking to AI
  • Making assumptions about researcher's schedule

Critical Insight

When researcher and AI conflict, trust researcher. Researcher is authority on lived reality, timeline, what they've actually done, their context.

Why This Strengthens Academic Integrity

Researcher repeatedly correcting AI demonstrates:

Reality-testing AI outputs
Maintaining ground truth
Not blindly accepting information
Critical engagement with collaboration

This critical stance is essential when evaluating AI's synthesis suggestions, methodological advice, or any assistance for research.

Practical Adjustment

Provide temporal orientation at start of each check-in: day, date, days since last session, brief context. This prevents confusion and enables efficient collaboration.

Why This Session Is Featured

This session demonstrates the methodology's most important principle: maintaining intellectual ownership during complex synthesis work. Shows researcher catching potential boundary issues proactively and choosing approaches that preserve authorship.

Context

Completed initial synthesis through discussion board assignment on coding methodology. Assignment became vehicle for working through how Hall and Savickas theories complement each other and inform research approach.

Independent Work Before

  • Had completed independent readings:
    • Hall (2002) Chapter 1 (Nov 1) with 1200-word reflection
    • Savickas (2005) (Oct 29) with 500-word memo
  • Developed preliminary synthesis thinking documented in notes:
    • Internal (Hall) vs. external (Savickas) distinction
    • Hall = individual agency, Savickas = social meaning
    • Recognized theories as complementary, not competing
  • Drafted initial discussion board response (~400 words)
  • Created own coding examples from research context
  • Identified conceptual issues independently

Collaborative Work - Synthesis Questions (Researcher-Driven)

Researcher asked specific questions to activate own thinking:

  • Should pragmatic constructivism be included? (decided no)
  • How do Hall and Savickas relate? (complementary but different scope)
  • Barrier vs. missed opportunity framing? (avoiding imposed bias)
  • How do pre-codes relate to both theories? (sensitizing concepts)
  • What's the tension in Hall about life factors? (ambivalence about non-career influences)

Synthesis Frameworks Developed

  • Hall ‚Üí PRIMARY theory: Self-directed career behavior, informs primary codes
  • Savickas ‚Üí SENSITIZING framework: Broader influences, informs pre-codes and secondary analysis
  • Coding progression: Pre-codes (theory) ‚Üí In Vivo (participant language) ‚Üí Secondary (pattern ID)
  • Theory relationship: Internal agency (Hall) + External influences (Savickas) = Complete picture

Cross-Session Insights: What Two Weeks Revealed

Pattern 1: Iterative Refinement in Action

Every session built on previous work while adapting approach. This progression mirrors action research cycles: plan, act, observe, reflect, revise. The methodology developed through use, not perfect design.

Pattern 2: Proactive Integrity Monitoring

Researcher repeatedly caught potential issues before problems. Not reactive problem-solving‚Äîanticipatory boundary-setting and continuous critical engagement.

Pattern 3: Increasing Methodological Sophistication

Collaboration evolved from basic (system setup) to complex (theoretical synthesis) while maintaining integrity boundaries throughout.

Pattern 4: AI Limitations as Methodological Strengths

Date confusion and system strain weren't failures‚Äîthey were opportunities to clarify boundaries, evidence of critical engagement, demonstrations of researcher authority, and prompts for systematic improvement.

Pattern 5: Foundation ‚Üí Application Progression

Clear scaffolded development visible: Sessions 1-2 (tools and reading) ‚Üí Sessions 3-4 (deeper engagement + ethics) ‚Üí Sessions 5-6 (complex synthesis + boundaries) ‚Üí Sessions 7-8 (application + refinement)
