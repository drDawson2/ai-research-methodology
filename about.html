About This Project

This methodology emerged from the intersection of doctoral research and professional practice. Having spent years facilitating professional development‚Äîcreating space for organic growth rather than directing outcomes‚ÄîI found myself applying the same principles to AI collaboration during dissertation preparation.

The same framework that worked for human facilitation applies to AI collaboration: provide structure, maintain agency, enable independence rather than dependence.

AI Philosophy: Enhancement, Not Replacement

This project's approach to AI integration is grounded in three core principles developed through years of professional practice and organizational observation.

Preserving Institutional Knowledge

Eliminating entry-level positions to "increase efficiency" destroys the pipeline of institutional knowledge. Early career employees learn how the workplace actually functions‚Äînot just tasks, but the why behind decisions and how systems interact.

AI can support task execution, but humans maintain the context and stakes that give work meaning.

Key insight: Products and services mean nothing if the knowledge about not only how to produce but WHY something is produced is lost.
üß†

Creating Cognitive Space

AI models can execute tasks more efficiently than humans in most cases. This creates opportunity for humans to do more meaningful work.

Added efficiency at the task level opens cognitive and temporal space for high-level thinking and creativity. Rather than threatening leadership, this represents opportunity for growth and innovation.

Key insight: If people spend more time strategizing and less time on necessary but not necessarily productive tasks, organizations gain human capacity for meaningful work.
üå±

Shifting the Narrative

Fear and defensive postures are the most stifling aspects of emerging technology. As soon as AI became widely available, narratives focused on job loss and misuse‚Äîand that's exactly what happened.

The correct approach: shift from fear and restriction to education and responsible use. Responsible use necessarily builds in mitigation because you cannot be responsible while engaging in irresponsible behavior.

Key insight: People with knowledge and access should work together to encourage responsible use through education, positive framing, and ethical modeling.

Why Share This (While It's Still Developing)

I've chosen to share this project even as it's in development. I know that not everyone has access to the things that I do, the knowledge and skills I've gained, or the comfort to pursue something that is a little scary.

Access & Equity

Not everyone has access to:

  • Institutional support for methodological exploration
  • Time to develop robust systems
  • Comfort with emerging technologies
  • Knowledge about how to use AI responsibly

Sharing detailed protocols and transparent documentation democratizes access to these approaches. Others can adapt this work without starting from scratch.

Narrative Shift Through Example

I'm tired of the negativity and disillusion that things cannot be different, and that there has to be a winning team in everything.

By modeling responsible use publicly, we shift the narrative from:

  • Fear ‚Üí Education
  • Restriction ‚Üí Responsible use
  • Zero-sum competition ‚Üí Collective learning

Learning From Every Experience

I've learned a lot from every experience I've had in my life, with other humans and now learning how to use this new tool, and I want to encourage others to do the same.

Transparency about process‚Äîincluding struggles, adjustments, and iterations‚Äîhelps others learn more effectively than polished final products.

If this work helps even one other researcher navigate AI collaboration more confidently and ethically, the transparency is worth it.

Methodological Positioning

This work draws on action research methodology and principles from facilitation practice:

Create Space for Organic Development

Just as I facilitated professional development by providing structure without directing outcomes, this AI methodology provides frameworks while maintaining researcher agency.

Facilitation Principle ‚Üí AI Application:

  • Hold space for exploration ‚Üí Structured prompts that activate thinking
  • Allow organic evolution ‚Üí Iterative refinement based on actual use
  • Prompt and prod, don't direct ‚Üí Questions that deepen analysis

Iterative Refinement Through Use

The methodology evolved through cycles:

  1. Build component
  2. Test in actual dissertation preparation
  3. Observe what works/doesn't work
  4. Reflect on effectiveness
  5. Revise and document
  6. Share learning

The consistency across contexts‚Äîfacilitating humans, collaborating with AI, conducting research‚Äîisn't coincidental. It's the same framework applied to different domains: create conditions for growth, maintain agency, build toward independence.
