Context

Creating public website to share AI collaboration methodology with other researchers. Goal: Make work accessible while maintaining transparency about website's own collaborative development.

Independent Work Before Collaboration

  • Identified need for shareable format beyond documents
  • Gathered all source materials (logs, frameworks, guides)
  • Developed branding guidelines (Autumn Forest color palette)
  • Made key structural decisions: multiple pages, left navigation, interactive elements needed
  • Determined audience (graduate students, researchers)
  • Established tone (conversationally technical)
  • Decided privacy approach (anonymize others, keep name for ownership)
  • Selected download formats (Markdown primary, HTML interactive, CSV for tracking)

Cross-Session Insights: What This Work Revealed

Pattern 1: Iterative Refinement in Action

Every session built on previous work while adapting approach. This progression mirrors action research cycles: plan, act, observe, reflect, revise. The methodology developed through use, not perfect design.

Pattern 2: Proactive Integrity Monitoring

Researcher repeatedly caught potential issues before problems. Not reactive problem-solving‚Äîanticipatory boundary-setting and continuous critical engagement.

Pattern 3: Increasing Methodological Sophistication

Collaboration evolved from basic (system setup) to complex (theoretical synthesis, public documentation) while maintaining integrity boundaries throughout.

Pattern 4: AI Limitations as Methodological Strengths

Date confusion and system strain weren't failures‚Äîthey were opportunities to clarify boundaries, evidence of critical engagement, demonstrations of researcher authority, and prompts for systematic improvement.

Pattern 5: Foundation ‚Üí Application ‚Üí Sharing Progression

Clear scaffolded development visible: Sessions 1-2 (tools and reading) ‚Üí Sessions 3-4 (deeper engagement + ethics) ‚Üí Sessions 5-6 (complex synthesis + boundaries) ‚Üí Sessions 7-8 (application + refinement) ‚Üí Session 9 (public documentation)

Two weeks of intensive development produced: Integrated research management system, academic integrity framework, theoretical synthesis capabilities, coding methodology protocols, quality control mechanisms, and this public documentation website.

This foundation enables ongoing work, but the methodology continues to develop as dissertation research progresses.
